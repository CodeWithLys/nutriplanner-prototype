Turn plastic waste into building blocks for your garden and classroom. Every bottle counts.

Why eco bricks?
🚯
Soft plastic can't be recycled
Chip packets, bread bags, and wrappers go straight to landfill — or end up in streets and rivers. Eco bricks give this plastic a useful second life.
🏗️
Free building material
Packed tightly, eco bricks are as strong as a regular brick. You can build garden borders, raised beds, benches, and even walls — at zero cost.
🧒
Children learn by doing
Making eco bricks teaches children about waste, responsibility, and the environment — and they can see the results in their own school garden.
💰
Earn extra income
Surplus bricks can be sold to builders, NGOs, and community projects — creating a small but steady income stream for your ECD centre.
What eco bricks look like
🧱🥤♻️
Plastic bottles packed tightly with clean, dry waste plastic
Eco bricks are 2-litre plastic bottles stuffed solid with non-recyclable soft plastic. When packed tightly enough, they become strong building units — used for garden borders, benches, and raised beds.

⚠️ Important before you start
All plastic must be clean and completely dry — no food residue, no moisture. Wet plastic causes mould inside the brick and it won't be usable.
How to build one
1
Collect & clean your plastics
Gather soft plastic waste — chip packets, bread bags, bubble wrap, straws, sweet wrappers. Wipe off any grease or food with a cloth. Leave to dry fully in the sun.
2
Start with a base layer
Add a small handful of colourful plastic into the empty bottle first — this creates a neat "window" effect and shows children what's inside.
3
Pack tightly with a stick
Push plastic in bit by bit using a dowel or thick stick. Pack each layer down firmly before adding more. The harder you pack, the stronger the brick. Rotate the bottle as you fill it.
4
Fill to the neck & weigh it
A standard 2-litre eco brick should weigh at least 200g when finished. If it feels light or squishy, keep packing. Label the bottle with your name and the date.
5
Seal & log your brick
Cap the bottle tightly. Bring it to the school and record it below — each brick earns your family points toward the garden bench project!
